Software Project-3

Cyclops Medtech: This Bangalore-based medtech company is building clinical and wearable diagnostic products for neuro-vestibular disorders using eye-tracking technology. BalanceEye, the product they have built, can detect dizziness, migraine, vertigo, and issues related to balance. It has been designed by oto-neurologists and is a patent-pending design. The company recently raised $1million in funding from Unitus Ventures, and is also supported by veteran investors like CP Bothra. BalanceEye is being used in several Tier2/3 towns in India as well as Europe, Middle East and parts of Asia. d eaque

sutainable Project-2

A lack of access to clean water is directly linked to hunger, health, and poverty. This is why startups develop solutions that provide access to underserved populations. These include solutions such as purifying water, harvesting rainwater as well as treating wastewater before it reaches water bodies. To ensure affordable and equitable access to drinking water, smart cities leverage technologies like IoT for efficient water management.

Moreover, clean energy and smart grids facilitate the wider adoption of electric vehicles (EVs)
